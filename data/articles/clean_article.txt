Financial regulators in the United Arab Emirates have launched a formal investigation into ABC Holdings Ltd, a Dubai-based conglomerate with significant operations across the Middle East. According to sources familiar with the matter, the probe centers on suspected violations of international trade sanctions and potential money laundering through a network of subsidiary companies.

The investigation was triggered by unusual wire transfers totaling approximately USD 4.7 million routed through accounts in Cyprus and the Cayman Islands between January and March 2026. Compliance officers at two major regional banks flagged the transactions after detecting patterns consistent with layering techniques commonly associated with sanctions evasion.

ABC Holdings, founded in 2008, operates in the energy, real estate, and import-export sectors. The company's director, Mohammed Al Rashid, has not been formally charged but is reportedly cooperating with investigators. Al Rashid denied any wrongdoing in a brief statement issued through the company's legal counsel.

UAE financial authorities have also requested cooperation from Swiss and Cypriot counterparts to trace the flow of funds through offshore corporate structures linked to the conglomerate.
